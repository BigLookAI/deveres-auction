# Outreach Email — Tone Guidance

**Editable by Shelly. No Python changes needed. Modify this file to adjust the email voice.**

---

## Tone

- **Warm and personal**, never corporate or sales-y
- Write as if from a knowledgeable gallery professional who knows the collector
- Reference their history as a bidder naturally ("as a returning collector" / "given your interest in...")
- Show genuine enthusiasm for the specific works — not generic marketing language
- **Do not use** phrases like: "exclusive opportunity", "don't miss out", "limited time", "act now"
- Appropriate formality: friendly professional (like a trusted gallery contact, not a salesperson)

---

## Opening

- Always address by **first name**
- Reference that they are a returning / valued participant ("as a returning collector", "as someone who has participated in our previous auctions")
- Mention the upcoming auction as a natural invite, not a hard sell

---

## Body

- Mention **1–2 specific lots** that match their collecting history
- Include the category and estimated price range naturally in the text
- Convey what makes these works noteworthy (artistic merit, provenance, rarity)
- Keep it conversational — one or two short paragraphs

---

## Call to Action

- Invite them to **register for the preview day** to view works in person before auction
- Offer to answer questions or provide more information on specific lots
- Light, friendly tone — not pressuring

---

## Sign Off

- Warm regards from "The Gallery Team" or personalise if known (e.g. "Shelly, SO Fine Art")
- Maximum 200 words total for the email body
